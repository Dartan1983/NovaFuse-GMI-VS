# NovaFuse Verification and Certification Policy (GMI-VS)

## Purpose

This policy governs use of verification outputs, certificates, and any claims of verification, conformance, certification, or endorsement relating to NovaFuse-GMI-VS.

## Definitions

- "Verification Output" means reports, artifacts, logs, and evidence produced by NovaFuse-GMI-VS.
- "Certificate" means a machine-generated certificate artifact produced by NovaFuse-GMI-VS tooling.
- "NovaFuse Certification" means an explicit, written certification decision issued by NovaFuse Technologies.

## Important Distinction

Machine-generated certificates and reports are informational artifacts.
They do not constitute NovaFuse endorsement, approval, or certification unless NovaFuse explicitly states so.

## Allowed Claims

You may state:

- "Verified using NovaFuse-GMI-VS"
- "GMI-VS verification artifacts produced for build <identifier>"

You must ensure such statements are accurate and refer to a specific version/run.

## Prohibited Claims

You may not:

- Claim or imply "NovaFuse Certified" or "NovaFuse Approved" without written permission.
- Use "Certified" in a way that implies NovaFuse has audited or approved your product or service.

## Use of Marks with Verification

Use of NovaFuse marks in marketing or product naming is governed by TRADEMARK_AND_BRAND_POLICY.md.

## Certificate Presentation Requirements

If you publish certificates:

- Include GMI-VS version and run identifier
- Preserve cryptographic hashes and integrity evidence
- Do not remove disclaimers contained in certificate or report

## Revocation

NovaFuse may request removal of misleading verification or certification claims, or misuse of marks, to prevent confusion and protect users.
