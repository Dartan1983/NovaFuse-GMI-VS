# NovaFuse Trademark and Brand Policy

## Purpose

NovaFuse Technologies provides certain software and specifications under open-source licenses. Those copyright licenses do not grant rights to NovaFuse trademarks, service marks, or logos.

This policy explains permitted and prohibited uses of NovaFuse marks to prevent confusion about origin, endorsement, certification, or affiliation.

## Covered Marks

This policy covers names and marks including (but not limited to):

- NovaFuse
- NovaFuse Verification Systems (VS)
- NovaFuse-GMI-VS
- NovaFuse-EPG-VS
- GMI-VS
- associated logos, icons, and visual identities

## General Rule

You may use NovaFuse marks only to make truthful, non-misleading statements about compatibility or origin, and only in ways that do not imply endorsement, sponsorship, or certification.

## Permitted Uses (Nominative Use)

You may:

- State that your product or service "uses", "integrates with", or is "compatible with" NovaFuse-GMI-VS.
- Refer to NovaFuse-GMI-VS to describe unmodified upstream project.
- Use word marks (not logos) to describe origin or compatibility.

## Prohibited Uses

You may not:

- Use NovaFuse marks in name of a fork, competing product, company name, domain name, or service.
- Use NovaFuse logos without explicit written permission.
- Present verification outputs or certificates in a way that implies NovaFuse endorsement or certification.
- Use confusingly similar names, logos, or branding that could cause user confusion.

## Modified Versions and Forks

If you distribute a modified version or fork:

- You must clearly indicate it is modified.
- You must not represent it as official NovaFuse release.
- You must remove NovaFuse logos from modified distributions unless you have written permission.

## Required Disclaimer (when relevant)

If you describe a product/service as compatible with NovaFuse marks, you must include:

"This product/service is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or certified by NovaFuse Technologies."
